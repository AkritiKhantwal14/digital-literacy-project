The DO’s 
•	Do use a professional profile picture on platforms like LinkedIn to ensure you are easily recognizable to recruiters and peers.
•	Do fact-check information from multiple reliable sources before sharing or "reposting" to prevent the spread of misinformation.
•	Do audit your privacy settings regularly to ensure that personal family photos or private thoughts aren't visible to the general public or future employers.
•	Do showcase your projects and achievements, such as hackathon wins or certificates, to build a "digital portfolio" of your hard work.
•	Do engage in constructive networking by following industry leaders and commenting thoughtfully on academic or professional topics.
The DON’Ts 
•	Don’t post confidential information, such as internal college documents, graded exam papers, or non-disclosure agreement (NDA) details from internships.
•	Don’t participate in "cyber-flaming" or heated public arguments; if you disagree with someone, handle it through a private, respectful message or let it go.
•	Don’t post content depicting unprofessional behavior, such as illegal activities or excessive partying, as these can be retrieved by recruiters years later.
•	Don’t tag others in compromising photos without their explicit permission, as this respects their digital boundaries and reputation.
•	Don’t use "text-speak" or slang when communicating with professors or professionals on platforms like LinkedIn or via direct messages.
