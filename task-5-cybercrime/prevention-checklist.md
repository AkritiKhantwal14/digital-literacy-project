Digital Ambassador’s Guide: Stay Safe Online
1.	PIN is for PAYING only: Remember that you never need to enter your UPI PIN to receive money. If someone asks for your PIN to send you a payment, it is a scam.
2.	Verify UPI IDs: Always double-check the recipient's name and UPI ID before hitting 'Send.' Scammers often use names that look like official brands (e.g., amazon.pay@okicici).
3.	Use 2-Factor Authentication (2FA): Enable 2FA on your email, social media, and WhatsApp to prevent account hijacking.
4.	Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Banking: Never log into your bank account or make UPI payments while connected to free, unsecured public Wi-Fi (like at cafes or railway stations).
5.	Check the "Lock" Icon: Ensure any website asking for personal info starts with https:// and has a padlock icon in the address bar.
6.	Don't Click Shortened Links: Be wary of SMS or WhatsApp messages with shortened URLs (e.g., bit.ly links) promising free recharges or rewards.
7.	Lock Your SIM Card: Set a SIM card PIN to prevent someone from receiving your OTPs if your phone is stolen.
8.	Update Software Regularly: Keep your phone’s OS and banking apps updated to protect against the latest security vulnerabilities.
HOW TO REPORT: If you are a victim of cybercrime, immediately call the National Helpline at 1930 and register a formal complaint at cybercrime.gov.in.
