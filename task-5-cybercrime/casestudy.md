Case Study: The "Refund" QR Code & UPI PIN Scam
What it is: Unified Payments Interface (UPI) fraud is a sophisticated form of social engineering where attackers trick victims into authorizing a debit transaction from their bank account under the false pretense of receiving a payment. A common and highly effective version is the "OLX Buyer" or "Customer Care Refund" scam.

How it happens (Step-by-Step): 1.  The Hook: The attacker identifies a target, often a student selling an item on a marketplace or someone who recently posted a complaint on social media about a failed food delivery. They pose as a legitimate buyer or a "Senior Executive" offering a pending refund.
2.  The Deception: To "process" the payment, the scammer sends a QR code via WhatsApp. They use high-pressure language, claiming the link will expire in minutes. They falsely state, "Scan this to receive the money directly into your bank account."
3.  The Trap: When the victim scans the code, their UPI app (GPay/PhonePe) opens. Crucially, the app prompts for a UPI PIN. The scammer remains on the call, insisting that the PIN is merely a "verification step" required by the bank to deposit the funds.
4.  The Theft: The moment the victim enters their PIN, they are actually authorizing a payment to the scammer. Money is instantly debited from their account.

Target Audience: College students and young professionals are the primary targets due to their high reliance on digital wallets for daily transactions and peer-to-peer commerce. Their comfort with technology often leads to a "false sense of security" that scammers exploit.

Consequences: The primary consequence is immediate financial loss, which is difficult to recover if the "Golden Hour" (the first two hours post-theft) is missed. Beyond the monetary hit, victims suffer from significant psychological distress and a long-term loss of trust in digital financial infrastructure, which can hinder their future digital participation.
